# Solomon — internal cover page

*Founder / strategy / advisor snapshot. Not for client distribution.*

---

## 1. Product + one-line positioning

**Solomon** — *Dedicated, managed AI chat and integrations: one isolated environment per customer, operated by us; optional multiple brands or sites inside that environment.*

---

## 2. What Solomon is

A **production-grade conversational layer** (chat, leads, events, webhooks, inbound integration APIs) delivered as **managed software**, not as a shared login-and-go SaaS. Each customer gets **their own deployment boundary**—data and runtime separated from other customers by default.

---

## 3. What problem it solves

| Pain | Outcome we sell |
|------|------------------|
| Teams want AI chat without owning LLM ops, queues, or databases | **We run the stack** they would otherwise duct-tape or overpay to build. |
| Multi-site or multi-brand orgs need separation without five products | **One dedicated environment**, multiple **internal** configurations (brands/sites) where needed. |
| Marketing/ops need leads and events in **their** tools | **Signed webhooks + integration endpoints**—automation-grade, not toy embeds. |
| “Just ship it” without a full platform hire | **Repeatable deploy + runbooks**—we productized the operator path. |

---

## 4. Who it is for

**Primary:** SMB and mid-market companies, **agencies** reselling one environment per end client, **multi-brand** businesses under one roof.

**Secondary (premium path):** Buyers who **must** host inside **their** cloud—**standalone install + services**, priced as control-first, not discount.

**Not the ICP:** Self-serve PLG, hyperscale multi-tenant SaaS where unrelated orgs share one footprint with zero per-customer ops.

---

## 5. Core offer / delivery model

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| **Default** | **1 customer = 1 dedicated Solomon environment** (managed). |
| **Internal scale** | Extra brands/sites = **optional tenants inside that same environment**—not pooling strangers. |
| **Primary SKU** | **Managed deployment**—we host and operate (standard). |
| **Exception** | **Standalone** in customer cloud—secondary, **higher** services risk; tight SOW. |
| **Access** | Admin/onboarding often tied to **existing platform SSO**; standalone deals need explicit access story. |

Tiers (**Base / Pro / Enterprise**) differ by **internal tenant count**, **integration depth**, **support**, **operational cadence** (e.g. monthly health summary in Pro), and **governance/SLA**—not by “which slice of a shared app.”

---

## 6. Pricing snapshot

**Economics:** Monthly must cover **their infra footprint + your labor + risk**—higher than “per-seat on shared SaaS.”

**AI spend:** Typically **customer’s OpenAI (or equivalent) account**—our fee is **platform + operations + support**, not tokens.

| Tier | Setup (indicative range) | Monthly (indicative range) |
|------|--------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Base** | $5k – $15k | $1.2k – $2.8k |
| **Pro** | $12k – $35k | $2.8k – $6.5k |
| **Enterprise** | $30k – $95k+ | $6k – $15k+ |

**Default first offer to lead with (“Dedicated managed chat — Base”):** ~**$8,500** setup + ~**$1,899**/mo — one dedicated environment, 1 prod + 1 staging internal tenant, two webhook destinations, standard go-live, email / next-business-day support.

**Pro reference package:** ~**$22k** setup + ~**$4.2k**/mo — same one environment, up to ~8 internal tenants, richer integrations, priority channel, monthly health summary.

**Extras (don’t give away):** major new integrations ($2k–$8k), after-hours retainer, second dedicated instance (e.g. DR), internal-tenant overage (~$75–$350/mo per extra tenant if you use that lever).

---

## 7. Why dedicated-instance-per-customer is strategically stronger

1. **Trust and procurement** — “Your environment” beats “you’re a row in our database” for serious buyers.
2. **Pricing power** — Infra + ops are **real costs**; the model **justifies** setup + recurring without apologizing.
3. **Clean liability story** — Isolation matches how **they** think about data; fewer awkward “who else is on this?” conversations.
4. **Agency resale** — One **Solomon per end client** is an easy **unit of sale** and renewal.
5. **Avoids a category trap** — We are **not** competing as generic cheap chat SaaS; we compete as **managed infrastructure + outcomes**.
6. **Honest ops** — The repo and runbooks **already** assume deploy-per-customer; the **commercial model matches the work**.

**Tradeoff (manage explicitly):** Margins compress if you **underprice** monthly or **over-service** small accounts—enforce **tier caps** and **standardized runbooks**.

## 8. One sentence (internal / external)

**You:** *“Solomon is a dedicated, managed AI chat and integration environment—we deploy and run one isolated stack per customer so they get production-grade chat and hooks into their systems without building or sharing a communal platform.”*

**They (buyer):** *“You get your own managed AI assistant on your site, connected to your tools, on infrastructure dedicated to your organization—not a shared app you sign up for online.”*

---

## 9. Key business model notes / what not to promise

**Do promise (when true):** Dedicated environment, managed go-live, ongoing operations per tier, webhooks and integration APIs as scoped, internal multi-brand inside **their** instance.

**Do not promise by default:** Self-serve signup, in-app billing, 24/7 phone without a retainer, unlimited integrations or tenants, compliance **certifications** you don’t hold, another company’s traffic in **their** instance, **their** OpenAI bill bundled as “all-in” unless you price it.

**Watch lines:** First-party admin SSO for every buyer without integration work; backups as “automatic product feature” unless you **operationalize** and **price** them; SLA percentages unless they match **real** hosting.

**Strategic discipline:** If someone wants **unrelated customers on one bill**, that’s a **different product decision**—margin, isolation, and support load must be **re-priced and re-architected in the contract**, not silently absorbed.
